# Phase 23 — Plan 01

**Title:** Matched quadratic-chirp 100m baseline
**Status:** Ready for execution
**Owner:** Session `M-matched100m`

## Goal

Reproduce the 100 m warm-start baseline, construct a pure quadratic chirp that
matches its broadening / peak-power trajectory as closely as possible, and
decide whether the warm-start's `-51.50 dB` transfer is mostly generic
pre-chirp or genuinely structure-dependent.

## Scope

- In scope:
  - Phase-owned `scripts/matched_*.jl`
  - `.planning/phases/23-matched-baseline/*`
  - results and standard images generated by the new driver
- Out of scope:
  - shared simulation / visualization source files
  - docs under `docs/*.tex`
  - multimode or HNLF follow-ups

## Tasks

1. Build the Phase 23 driver in `scripts/matched_*.jl`.
   It must load the warm-start artifact, reconstruct the trusted 100 m problem,
   rerun the warm-start propagation, and expose a pure-quadratic baseline path.

2. Implement a threaded `a₂` sweep.
   Use `Threads.@threads` with `deepcopy(fiber)` per thread, evaluate a coarse
   chirp grid spanning both signs, and store per-point diagnostics plus
   standard images.

3. Define the matching metric and select the best quadratic.
   Use a trajectory mismatch metric based on peak power versus `z` if
   available; otherwise fall back to a temporal-width / endpoint proxy that is
   logged explicitly in the output.

4. Validate numerical trust.
   For every candidate discussed in the verdict, report:
   - `J_dB`
   - boundary-condition edge fraction
   - energy drift
   Reject or rerun any candidate with `edge fraction >= 1e-3`.

5. Generate the visual comparison package.
   Emit the mandatory standard image sets and one overlay plot comparing the
   warm-start and matched-quadratic spectral evolutions.

6. Write the phase summary.
   Produce `.planning/phases/23-matched-baseline/SUMMARY.md` with the headline
   numbers, the visual verdict, and a docs-ready one-paragraph conclusion.

## Verification

- The warm-start rerun reproduces approximately `-51.50 dB` on the trusted grid.
- The chosen quadratic baseline is a pure quadratic phase, not a polynomial fit
  plus residual.
- Standard images exist on disk for the warm-start rerun and the matched
  quadratic baseline.
- The summary states one of three verdicts only:
  - within `~3 dB`: mostly pre-chirp
  - `>=10 dB` worse: structure matters
  - otherwise: partially explained by pre-chirp

## Risks

- The stored artifact schema may differ from Session F's earlier files.
  Mitigation: inspect keys directly and make the driver tolerant to minor field
  naming differences.
- A naive quadratic sweep could accidentally choose an aliased candidate.
  Mitigation: couple selection to the trust metrics, not just `J_dB`.
- Matching `P(z)` may require z-saved forward runs for many candidates.
  Mitigation: use a coarse sweep first, then rerun only the best few with dense
  `zsave`.
