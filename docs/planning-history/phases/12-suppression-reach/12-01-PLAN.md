<objective>
Long-fiber propagation: take existing best phi_opt from L=0.5m and L=2m optimizations, interpolate onto a larger frequency grid, and propagate through L=10m and L=30m fibers with 100 z-save points. Compare shaped vs flat phase at every z-position. Answer the key question: at 10x-60x the optimization length, does phi_opt still provide ANY benefit over flat phase?

Purpose: Characterize the finite reach of spectral phase Raman suppression — the user's physical intuition is that single-pass input shaping has a finite reach, and we need to quantify how that reach decays with distance.

Output: `scripts/propagation_reach.jl` (PR_ prefix), 8 JLD2 files in `results/raman/phase12/`, 3 diagnostic figures in `results/images/`.
</objective>
