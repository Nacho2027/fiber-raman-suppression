# Phase 24 Plan 02 — Corrective Pass on the Overnight-Integration Docs

## Objective

Plan 24-01 (commit `6b4dadb`) landed the overnight results into the
canonical `.tex` files but left behind three classes of defect that
24-RESEARCH-external.md documented after the fact:

1. **Factual misquotes.** The companion's Overnight-update box misattributes
   a Phase-22 reproduction to Phase 21, and §sec:april-sharpness recommends
   a Pareto-57 trade that a glance at the data shows is dominated.
2. **Missing physics / literature context.** The Phase 23 retraction prose
   describes the effect but not the mechanism; there is no Mamyshev-oscillator
   anchor; the SAM null result is framed as "nothing happened" instead of as
   the expected behaviour for a deterministic gauge-degenerate saddle; and
   the trH mechanism claim is not properly caveated for an indefinite Hessian.
3. **Housekeeping bugs.** An unregistered yellow+orange tcolorbox collides
   with the `advisory` palette, an undefined `\ref{sec:gauge}` survived from
   Phase 20, and a staged PNG (`phase21_100m_phase_profile.png`) is present
   on disk but never `\includegraphics`'d.

This plan is the single corrective commit. It does NOT rerun any
simulation; every number it introduces is pasted from the prompt
(which sourced them from the three sibling SUMMARYs and the external
research doc). At the end, a physics appendix
(`24-PHYSICS-VERIFICATION.md`) sits alongside the SUMMARY with the
full soliton-number and pre-chirp arithmetic in explicit form.

All work happens on branch `sessions/D-docs` in worktree
`~/raman-wt-docs`. Files modified are limited to `docs/*.tex`,
`docs/*.pdf`, and the two markdown files in the phase directory.
No source code, no `.planning/STATE.md`, no `.planning/ROADMAP.md`.

### R3 — trH indefinite-Hessian caveat

**File.** `docs/verification_document.tex` §sec:april-sharpness,
immediately after the `keyresult` box (L1512–1520).

**Change.** Insert a new `advisory` block:

```latex
\begin{advisory}
\textbf{Caveat on the flat-basin claim.} The published trH-regularisation
literature (Liu, \emph{Neurocomputing} 536, arXiv:2208.05924, 2023; Ju,
\emph{Noise Stability Optimization}, arXiv:2306.08553, 2023) assumes a
positive-semidefinite Hessian: on a non-negative loss, penalising
$+\lambda \cdot \mathrm{Tr}(H)$ pushes the optimiser toward flatter
\emph{positive-curvature} directions. Our Hessian is \emph{indefinite}
($|\lambda_\text{min}|/\lambda_\text{max} \approx 2.6\%$ SMF, Phase 13).
On an indefinite spectrum, $\mathrm{Tr}(H) = \sum_i \lambda_i$ is signed
and can be reduced in two ways: \emph{(a)} by shrinking positive
eigenvalues (the intended effect --- a genuinely flatter basin in the
positive-curvature subspace), or \emph{(b)} by sharpening negative
```

4. **Chirped sech² duration.** Give the standard formula
   $T_\text{chirped} = \sqrt{T_0^2 + (\text{GDD}/T_0)^2}$ (stating
   it is the RMS-equivalent for Gaussian and differs from the sech²
   exact result by an O(1) prefactor). For GDD $= +4\times 10^{-24}$~s²
   and $T_0 = 1.05\times 10^{-13}$~s:
   $\text{GDD}/T_0 = 3.81\times 10^{-11}$~s, and
   $T_\text{chirped} \approx 3.81\times 10^{-11}$~s $= 38$~ps.
   Stretch factor $\approx 362\times$.

5. **Peak power rescaling.** Energy conservation: $P_\text{peak}
   \cdot T_0 \approx $ const (to leading order for a linearly chirped
   pulse with no nonlinear reshaping). Hence
   $P_\text{peak,stretched} = P_\text{peak} / 362 \approx 8$~W.
   State the assumption explicitly (no nonlinear reshaping during
   pre-chirp, which is correct because the pulse shaper is pre-fiber).

6. **Integrated nonlinear phase over 100 m of the stretched pulse.**
   $\Phi_\text{NL} = \int_0^L \gamma P_\text{peak}(z) \, dz$. For a
   stretched pulse with slowly-varying peak power through the 100 m
   fiber (peak stays $\mathcal{O}(10)$~W throughout because
   $|\beta_2| L = 2.17$~ps$^2$ only partially compensates $+4$~ps$^2$
   of pre-chirp), $P_\text{peak,avg} \approx 10$~W (order-of-magnitude).
   So $\Phi_\text{NL} \approx \gamma \cdot 10 \text{~W} \cdot 100 \text{~m}
   = 1.3\times 10^{-3} \cdot 10 \cdot 100 = 1.3$~rad.
   Compare to canonical short-fiber case ($L{=}0.5$~m, $P{=}0.05$~W
   unchirped): $\Phi_\text{NL} = \gamma P_\text{peak,unchirped} L =
   1.3\times 10^{-3} \cdot 2959 \cdot 0.5 = 1.92$~rad (the audit's
   independent $1.63$~rad differs by an O(1) prefactor that is
   irrelevant for the argument). Both setups within a factor of $\sim 2$
   of each other --- confirming the "same Φ\_NL budget spread over
   $200\times$ the length at $\sim 1/30$ the instantaneous intensity"
   picture.

7. **Why Raman gain is below threshold.** Raman gain exponent
   $g \cdot P_\text{peak} \cdot L_\text{eff}$ with
   $g_R(\Omega_R) \approx 1\times 10^{-13}$ m/W at the 13.2~THz
   Stokes peak in silica (textbook value). For stretched peak power
   10~W over 100~m: exponent $= 10^{-13} \cdot 10 \cdot 100 = 10^{-10}$,
   absolutely negligible --- Raman gain never crosses the $\sim 16$
   threshold that separates spontaneous from stimulated regime. For
   unchirped peak power 2959~W: exponent would be $\approx 3\times 10^{-8}$
   over 100~m, still below threshold but only because the pulse is
   short; the relevant Raman action is not exponential gain but
   integrated Raman-response conv integral --- mention this and
   reference `hRω` in the code for the detailed form. The Raman
   gain argument is a quick sanity check, not the full story.

8. **Walk-off arithmetic (100 m audit check).** Group-velocity
   mismatch between pump at $\omega_0$ and Stokes at
   $\omega_0 - 2\pi \cdot 13.2$~THz is $|\beta_2| \cdot \Delta\omega_R$.

   $= 8.29\times 10^{13}$ rad/s and $|\beta_2| = 2.17\times 10^{-26}$
   s²/m: $|\beta_2| \Delta\omega_R = 1.80\times 10^{-12}$ s/m

   $L = 0.5$~m: walk-off $= 0.9$~ps. State clearly: Phase 16's
